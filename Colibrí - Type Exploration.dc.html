a carved display face
A Latin display alphabet drawn from the visual language of Taíno petroglyphs and pictographs — incised, frontal, symmetrical. Built for titles and the logotype; never for body text.
a note on authenticity
The Taíno carved and painted symbols — faces, frogs, spirals, the cemí spirit-forms — not a written alphabet. So there is no script to revive. What we design here is an original Latin face that borrows the hand of that mark-making. The ornament glyphs below are bespoke; the letters are a first cut, open to your eye.
01
Carved, not typed
Strokes read as if cut into stone or wood — even weight, blunt terminals, a hand-cut steadiness rather than mechanical precision.
02
Frontal & symmetrical
Taíno carving faces you head-on. The face is even and open, built on a calm geometric grid that looks you in the eye.
03
The wrapped eye
Round counters carry a concentric ring — the petroglyph eye — so an O or a C looks back at the reader.
