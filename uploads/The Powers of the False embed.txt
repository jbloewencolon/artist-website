Description:

This project arose from my failure to make sense of Deleuze’s cinema books outside of their cinematic context. Initially, my goal was to create a video archive of all the different films and scenes Deleuze mentions in his chapter on the powers of the false in order to provide a visual supplement to the text. It quickly became apparent, however, that these clips would only add to the confusion. So, rather than dealing with concepts, I decided to make a video that creates an event. One that is focused on generating affects in regards to Deleuze’s concept. Rather than explaining the powers of the false through rhetoric and argument, I sought to explain it through form and experience. Talking about the powers of the false is not enough, they need to be seen, felt, and heard.

Stylistically, I limited myself to drawing cinematic material from two films only, Orson Welles’ "F for Fake", and the documentary "Gilles Deleuze: From A to Z." Manipulating clips from both allowed me to create two fictional characters “The Artiste” and “The Forger” who put on the guise of both Deleuze and Welles in order to become powers of the false themselves. By having the virtual Deleuze attempt to articulate the powers of the false, I hope I have gotten closer to their actuality. I embedded it all within a fictional narrative generated by the virtual Welles that was capable of lucidly deceiving the audience much like his actual film.
